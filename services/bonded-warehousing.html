What is Bonded Warehousing?

A bonded warehouse is a secure facility where imported goods can be stored without payment of customs duties until they are released for consumption or re-exported. This service helps businesses manage cash flow and optimize their supply chain. We are currently in the planning phase for our bonded warehousing facilities.

Deferred Duty Payment

Pay customs duties only when goods are released from the warehouse, improving cash flow.

Re-export Options

Goods can be re-exported from the bonded warehouse without paying import duties.

Inventory Management

Professional inventory tracking and management systems for your stored goods.

Secure Storage

24/7 security monitoring and controlled access to protect your valuable cargo.

Inventory Processing

Services including sorting, labeling, and repacking within the bonded facility.

Customs Documentation

Complete documentation support for all warehouse transactions and customs requirements.

Key Features (Planned)

  • 24/7 security monitoring
  • Climate-controlled options available
  • Documentation support

Benefits of Bonded Warehousing

Cash Flow Optimization

Defer duty payments until goods leave the warehouse, improving working capital.

Supply Chain Flexibility

Store goods closer to markets and respond quickly to demand changes.

International Trade

Ideal for goods in transit or awaiting distribution to multiple markets.

Strategic Timing

Time your duty payments and market entry for optimal business advantage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a bonded warehouse?

A customs-bonded warehouse is a secure facility where imported goods can be stored without payment of duties until they are released.

How long can goods stay in bond?

Typically goods can remain in a bonded warehouse for up to 5 years, depending on customs regulations.

Can I process goods in a bonded warehouse?

Yes, certain manipulations like sorting, labeling, and repacking are usually permitted under customs supervision.

What happens to damaged goods?

Procedures exist for handling damaged or destroyed goods, which may affect duty liability.

How do I withdraw goods from bond?

Goods can be withdrawn for home consumption (paying duty) or for re-export (duty-free).

Is bonded warehousing right for me?

If you import goods and want to defer duty payments, bonded warehousing could benefit your business.
